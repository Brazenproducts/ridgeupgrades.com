Kawasaki Ridge Upgrades — What Owners Are Modding First

Real owner insights on the most impactful mods for the world's first full-time 4WD side-by-side — ranked by popularity and value.

The Kawasaki Ridge arrived in 2024 as a genuine disruption in the utility side-by-side market. Its 999cc parallel-twin, 100-horsepower engine paired with the industry's first standard full-time 4WD drivetrain gave buyers a machine that simply grips and goes without driver intervention. As the platform has matured through its first full ownership cycle, a clear picture has emerged of which upgrades Ridge owners are prioritizing — and why.

This isn't a theoretical wish list. These are the specific mods appearing most frequently in Ridge owner forums, dealer conversations, and buyer reviews. We break down what each upgrade costs, what problem it solves, and whether it's worth your money based on how you use the machine.

Mod #1 — The Windshield Upgrade

🔥 Most Popular First Mod

Full Polycarbonate Vented Windshield

Across every Ridge owner community, the windshield upgrade is the single most discussed modification. The stock windshield configuration works but leaves many owners wanting more dust and debris protection, especially for agricultural and trail use where roost from front tires is constant.

Aftermarket full windshields in scratch-resistant polycarbonate offer a dramatic improvement. Vented upper panels allow airflow at low speeds without opening the cab to debris. The best models integrate directly with the Ridge's OEM mounting points — no drilling required.

  • Scratch-resistant hard coat protects long-term clarity
  • Vented designs balance airflow and protection
  • OEM mount compatibility means clean installation
  • Average installation time: 45 minutes

Mod #2 — Hard Roof Panel

OEM-Style Hard Roof Panel

The roof mod is the second most popular upgrade on the Ridge, typically installed within the first two months of ownership. A hard roof panel dramatically changes the ridability of the machine in sun and rain — and it creates a mounting surface for light bars, antennas, and storage solutions that soft tops cannot support.

Kawasaki offers an OEM hard roof for the Ridge, but aftermarket manufacturers have also brought aluminum and high-density poly panels to market at competitive price points. If you plan to mount a light bar on the roof (Mod #4 below), ensure your roof panel is rated for the weight and has integrated mounting channels or tabs.

  • Reduces cab temperature significantly on sunny days
  • Provides a stable platform for light and antenna mounts
  • Aluminum options are lighter than poly for performance builds
  • OEM options ensure perfect ROPS integration

Mod #3 — Door Kit Installation

Doors are a comfort and safety upgrade that transforms how the Ridge feels on long rides and in cold weather. The open-cab experience is great in mild conditions, but Ridge owners who work in rain, wind, or temperatures below 50°F almost universally add doors within their first season.

The most popular door configuration is aluminum half-doors with integrated net windows. These bolt to the existing cab structure and add meaningful side protection without the weight and claustrophobia of full glass or poly doors. For work applications, aluminum doors also resist UV degradation and won't crack in cold weather like some plastic alternatives.

Full door kits with mesh or solid lower panels and zip-out windows are available for maximum weather protection. These are most popular in northern climates and for winter ranching operations where mud and cold are constant factors.

Mod #4 — LED Light Bar System

Curved 40–50 Inch LED Roof Bar

Lighting upgrades are the fourth most common Ridge mod, and for good reason — anyone running the machine before sunrise or after sunset quickly discovers how limiting stock lighting can be at working speeds. A quality 40 to 50 inch curved LED bar transforms nighttime visibility.

Curved bars are preferred over straight bars on the Ridge because the curvature delivers better peripheral light coverage for scanning terrain and tree lines. Install on the front roof bracket with a waterproof relay harness connected directly to the battery. This approach keeps the electrical draw off the factory wiring and adds a physical switch that owners can control independently from the key position.

  • 40" bar: good for trail riding and moderate work use
  • 50" bar: ideal for farm/field work requiring wide coverage
  • Look for IP68 waterproofing and 10,000+ lumen output
  • Combo beam (spot + flood) is most versatile for mixed use

Mod #5 — Cargo and Bed Upgrades

The Ridge's cargo bed is a genuine strength of the platform, and smart owners invest early in bed management accessories that make that space more functional. The most popular cargo mods in order of adoption rate are:

  1. Heavy-duty bed mat: Prevents cargo from sliding and protects the bed surface from scratches and dents. Contoured rubber mats with raised edges are better than flat liner sheets.
  2. Cargo net system: A fitted cargo net with adjustable anchor points keeps loose items from bouncing out at speed. Essential for trail use where the full-time 4WD allows aggressive driving speeds.
  3. Bed extender: For carrying long items — lumber, pipe, fence posts — a rear bed extender adds length without requiring a trailer. Most models mount to the existing bed receiver or tailgate.
  4. Tool holder mounts: Rail-mount systems allow modular attachment of tool holders, gun scabbards, and gear organizers without drilling into the bed.

Mod #6 — Skid Plates and Underbody Protection

Full-time 4WD lets Ridge owners push into terrain that 2WD/4WD switchable machines avoid. That capability translates directly into a higher probability of rock strikes and log impacts on the undercarriage. Smart Ridge builders add skid plate protection early — before the first scrape, not after.

Engine skid plates, transmission guards, and fuel tank shields are available as complete kit systems. UHMW polyethylene slides over rocks without damage and absorbs impacts effectively for the price. Aluminum plate systems offer superior protection for true rock-crawling applications at a higher weight penalty.

Frequently Asked Questions — Kawasaki Ridge Mods

What is the first mod most Kawasaki Ridge owners make?

Most Ridge owners start with a full windshield upgrade. The OEM windshield is functional but many owners want better debris and weather protection, making aftermarket poly windshields the single most popular first mod.

Does the Kawasaki Ridge have a roof option?

Yes, both OEM and aftermarket hard and soft roof panels are available for the Ridge. Hard roofs offer better durability and can double as a mounting platform for lights or cargo.

Can I add doors to the Kawasaki Ridge?

Aftermarket half and full door kits are available for the Ridge. Aluminum doors with integrated net windows are the most popular option for trail riders wanting side protection without a full cab enclosure.

What light bar size fits the Kawasaki Ridge?

Most Ridge owners run a 40- to 50-inch curved LED light bar on the front roof bracket. The Ridge's electrical system comfortably handles the additional draw with a proper relay harness.

Are cargo upgrades worth it on the Kawasaki Ridge?

Absolutely. The Ridge's full-time 4WD means you push harder into terrain, and a good cargo management system — rubber mat, tie-downs, cargo divider — ensures your gear arrives where you do.

What skid plate options exist for the Kawasaki Ridge?

UHMW poly and aluminum skid plate kits are available for the Ridge's engine, transmission, and fuel tank. Many owners add full-coverage plate systems for aggressive terrain use.

Can I add a winch to the Kawasaki Ridge?

Yes. The Ridge's front bumper structure accommodates winch mounts designed for 3,000 to 4,500 lb capacity winches. Synthetic rope is strongly recommended over steel cable for safety.

How difficult are Kawasaki Ridge upgrades to install?

Most bolt-on accessories like windshields, roofs, light bars, and cargo accessories require basic hand tools and take 30 to 90 minutes to install. Winch installations may require more time and electrical work.

Do aftermarket upgrades void my Kawasaki Ridge warranty?

Bolt-on accessories that don't modify the drivetrain or engine generally don't affect warranty coverage. Consult your dealer for specific guidance on any modification that interacts with powertrain components.

Prioritizing Your Ridge Upgrade Budget

With a finite budget, the question isn't what to add — it's what to add first. Based on owner feedback and the Ridge's specific use-case profile, we recommend this upgrade priority sequence:

  1. Windshield — Daily quality-of-life improvement, installed immediately
  2. Skid plates — Preventive protection before the first demanding trail
  3. Hard roof — Weather protection and light mounting platform
  4. LED light bar — Enables pre-dawn and post-sunset work and recreation
  5. Doors — Comfort upgrade for cold, wet, or dusty conditions
  6. Cargo system — Maximizes the bed's working potential

Every Ridge owner's priorities are different. A farmer running the machine six days a week in Montana has different needs than a weekend trail rider in North Carolina. But this priority order represents the best value-per-dollar investment sequence for the widest range of Ridge buyers.
